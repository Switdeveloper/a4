FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about commercial insurance in Florida.

Requirements vary by business type and industry. Generally, workers' compensation is mandatory for businesses with 4+ employees (construction requires it regardless). Commercial auto is required for business vehicles. General liability and commercial property are typically required by landlords or lenders. Our agents will help you identify all mandatory coverages for your specific business.

Costs vary dramatically based on business type, revenue, employee count, claims history, location, and coverage limits. Small businesses might pay $500-$2,000 annually for basic coverage, while larger operations can pay tens of thousands. We comparison shop 40+ carriers to find the most competitive rates for your specific business profile.

General Liability (GL) covers physical damage and injury claims — slip and fall accidents, property damage, bodily injury, and advertising injury. Professional Liability (E&O) covers financial harm caused by professional advice or services — negligence, errors, omissions, and failure to deliver promised services. Most businesses need GL; professional services need both.

Yes. If you or your employees use personal vehicles for business purposes (deliveries, client meetings, transporting tools/equipment), you need commercial auto insurance. Personal auto policies typically exclude business use and could deny claims if an accident occurs while conducting business. Commercial auto provides higher liability limits and proper coverage for business activities.

Absolutely! A Business Owner's Policy (BOP) bundles general liability and commercial property. You can also bundle commercial auto, workers' comp, and other coverages with the same carrier for package discounts. Bundling simplifies billing, reduces gaps in coverage, and typically saves 10-25% on premiums. We'll design the optimal package for your business.

Cyber liability covers costs from data breaches, hacking, ransomware attacks, and other cyber incidents. It includes: customer notification costs, credit monitoring, forensic investigation, business interruption losses, data restoration, regulatory fines, and legal defense costs. Essential for any business storing customer data, processing payments, or handling sensitive information.
