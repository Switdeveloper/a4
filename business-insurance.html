COVERAGE OPTIONS

What's Covered in Your Business Policy

A standard business insurance policy includes these coverages, with optional add-ons for tailored protection.

General Liability Insurance

Covers third-party claims of bodily injury, property damage, and advertising injury. Essential for all businesses.

Workers' Compensation

Required for Florida businesses with 4+ employees. Covers medical expenses and lost wages for work-related injuries.

Commercial Auto Insurance

Covers business-owned vehicles, delivery fleets, and employee-driven vehicles. Required for company vehicles.

Business Owner's Policy (BOP)

Bundles general liability and commercial property into one affordable policy. Ideal for small to medium businesses.

Commercial Property Insurance

Protects your business buildings, equipment, inventory, and furniture from fire, theft, and natural disasters.

Professional Liability (E&O)

Covers claims of negligence, errors, or omissions for professional service providers like consultants, lawyers, and doctors.

Directors & Officers (D&O)

Protects corporate directors and officers from personal losses if sued for alleged wrongful acts in managing the company.

Cyber Liability Insurance

Covers data breaches, cyber attacks, and other technology-related risks. Essential for businesses handling customer data.

Commercial Umbrella

Provides additional liability coverage beyond the limits of your primary policies for extra protection.

Inland Marine Insurance

Covers property in transit, tools and equipment, and other movable business property not covered by standard policies.

Liquor Liability Insurance

Required for businesses that manufacture, sell, or serve alcohol. Covers alcohol-related incidents and injuries.

Product Liability Insurance

Protects manufacturers, distributors, and retailers from claims related to product defects or malfunctions.
