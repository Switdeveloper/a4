COVERAGE OPTIONS

What's Covered in Your Life Policy

Life insurance provides financial protection for your loved ones. Different policies offer various benefits to match your goals.

Term Life Insurance

Pure protection for a set period (10, 20, or 30 years). Pays death benefit if you pass away during the term. Most affordable option.

Whole Life Insurance

Permanent coverage that lasts your entire life. Includes guaranteed death benefit, cash value growth, and fixed premiums.

Universal Life Insurance

Flexible premium and adjustable death benefit. Cash value grows based on interest rates; you can adjust coverage as needed.

Final Expense Insurance

Small, affordable policy to cover funeral costs and final medical bills. Easier qualification, often no medical exam required.

Mortgage Protection Insurance

Pays off your mortgage if you die before it's paid off. Ensures your family can stay in their home without financial stress.

Accidental Death & Dismemberment

Additional coverage that pays if you die or suffer dismemberment from an accident. Often added as a rider to existing policies.

Children's Life Insurance Riders

Add coverage for your children at a low cost. Provides a death benefit if the unexpected happens and can often be converted to adult coverage later.

Waiver of Premium Rider

Waives your premium payments if you become disabled and unable to work. Keeps your coverage active during tough times.

Accelerated Death Benefit

Allows you to access a portion of the death benefit if diagnosed with a terminal illness. Use funds for medical expenses or quality of life.

Return of Premium Option

If you outlive your term policy, get back all the premiums you paid. More expensive, but offers savings if you survive the term.

No Medical Exam Policies

Simplified issue life insurance requires only health questions; no physical exam. Coverage decisions often in minutes. Lower face amounts typically.

Guaranteed Issue Life Insurance

No medical questions or exams. Acceptance guaranteed (typically ages 50-85). Smaller face amounts, but guaranteed coverage for final expenses.

Whole life provides permanent coverage for your entire life, includes guaranteed cash value growth, and has fixed premiums. It's more expensive but builds financial assets over time.

Generally, life insurance death benefits paid to beneficiaries are not considered taxable income by the IRS, so your family receives the full amount tax-free. However, if the policy has a cash value component and you surrender it for more than your basis (total premiums paid), that gain may be taxable. Estate taxes may also apply to very large policies (over $12.06 million in 2022).
